# Medical Disclaimer

## Experimental Software

DxEngine is an **experimental research project** built entirely by AI (Claude), directed by a non-medical-professional with no programming or medical background. All code, algorithms, medical knowledge curation, and evaluation design was performed by a large language model. The medical data was extracted from published literature by AI - **not** by a clinician, medical expert, or trained programmer. Nothing in this project has been reviewed or validated by a medical board, clinical experts, or any peer-review process.

## Not a Medical Device

DxEngine is **not** a medical device, has **not** been cleared or approved by the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA), the European Medicines Agency (EMA), or any other regulatory body, and is **not** certified under the EU Medical Device Regulation (MDR) or any equivalent framework.

## Not Medical Advice

DxEngine does **not** provide medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment recommendations. The output of this software - including ranked differential diagnoses, probability estimates, and test recommendations - is generated by algorithmic processing of curated medical data and should **never** be used as the basis for clinical decisions.

## Not Clinically Validated

This software has been tested only against synthetic and teaching-case evaluations created by the same non-medical-professional who built the system. It has **never** been validated on real patient data, in clinical settings, or through any formal clinical study. The evaluation numbers reported in the documentation reflect performance on curated test cases, not real-world clinical accuracy.

## Intended Use

This software is intended exclusively for:
- **Researchers** studying clinical decision support systems, Bayesian diagnostic methods, or medical AI
- **Medical students and educators** as a teaching aid for understanding probabilistic diagnostic reasoning - with the understanding that the medical content has not been expert-reviewed
- **Software developers** interested in hybrid AI architectures combining deterministic and LLM reasoning

This software is **not** intended for:
- Clinical decision-making of any kind
- Patient self-diagnosis
- Emergency medical triage or treatment decisions
- Use as a substitute for professional medical consultation
- Any situation where a person's health or safety depends on the output

## Known Limitations

DxEngine has documented limitations that users must understand:
- Covers **54 diseases** out of thousands of possible diagnoses
- Reference ranges are derived from predominantly **North American/European populations** and may not be appropriate for all patient populations (see [Equity Audit](docs/EQUITY_AUDIT.md))
- Likelihood ratios are sourced from published medical literature that may not represent all demographics equally
- Validated on **teaching cases**, not real patient data from clinical settings
- Does not account for medication effects, drug interactions, or complex comorbidities beyond its disease vocabulary
- Does not interpret medical imaging, pathology, microbiology cultures, or genetic testing

For a complete assessment of the system's safety profile, see the [Safety Argument](docs/SAFETY_ARGUMENT.md).
